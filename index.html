        Pre-trip checklist

        • Current fishing license on you (plus Lake Erie permit if fishing Erie or its tributaries)
        • Checked seasons & bag/size limits for this water on ODNR
        • Life jacket per person; check the marine/lake forecast for wind & storms
        • Tell someone your launch point and return time
        • Clean, drain, dry gear between waters — stop invasive species

        Angler's glossary

        Plain-language terms
        Structure
        The permanent shape of the bottom — drop-offs, humps, points, channels. Fish relate to it.
        Cover
        Things fish hide in or under — weeds, logs, docks, rocks, brush.
        Drop-off
        Where shallow water falls quickly into deep. A highway and an ambush spot.
        Point
        A finger of land or bottom reaching into the water; it concentrates fish and bait.
        Flat
        A wide, shallow, fairly even area — good in spring and at low light.
        Windward shore
        The bank the wind blows toward. Baitfish pile up there, and so do feeding fish.
        Chop
        Light wind-driven surface waves. Breaks up the light and often turns fish on.
        Falling pressure
        Barometer dropping as a front moves in — frequently the best bite window.
        Low light
        Dawn, dusk, and overcast — prime feeding times for most species.
        Jig
        A weighted hook, often dressed with soft plastic or live bait, bounced near bottom. The do-everything lure.
        Crankbait
        A diving plug you reel steadily to cover water and trigger reaction strikes.
        Slip bobber
        A float that slides on the line so you can fish bait at a set depth. Beginner gold.
        Total length
        How fish are measured for limits: closed mouth to pinched tail tip, laid flat.
        Minimum & slot
        Size rules: a minimum means keep only fish that long or longer; a slot protects a mid-size range.
        Catch and release
        Returning fish unharmed — wet your hands, support the belly, get it back in fast.
